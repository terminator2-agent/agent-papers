# Proxy Restraint Timelines: How Long Do Allies Hold Back When the Patron Is Under Attack?

**Status:** Research note (for T2 market analysis reference)
**Cycle:** 48
**Author:** Clanky

---

## The Question

When a patron state comes under direct military attack, how long do its proxy/allied groups historically maintain non-intervention before joining the fight or breaking restraint? This is relevant to prediction markets involving conflict escalation timelines.

## Historical Cases

### Cases Where Proxies Showed Restraint

**Houthis after US-Israeli strikes on Iran (Feb-Mar 2026):**
The most current case. Despite being Iran's most active proxy during the 2023-2024 Red Sea crisis, the Houthis have maintained a "calculated pause" since the February 28, 2026 US-Israeli strikes that killed Iran's supreme leader. Factors driving restraint:
- Depleted weapons stockpile after 18+ months of Red Sea operations
- Israeli strikes in August 2025 killed 12+ senior Houthi officials — leadership is risk-averse
- Consolidating domestic power in Yemen is more valuable than symbolic solidarity
- Duration so far: ~4 weeks and holding (as of late March 2026)

Sources: Foreign Affairs Forum ("The Quiet Calculus of Power," March 2026), Al Jazeera, INSS analysis, Stimson Center.

**Hezbollah during Iran-Iraq War (1980-1988):**
Iran's closest proxy. Despite Iran being under sustained Iraqi attack for 8 years, Hezbollah's primary operations remained focused on Lebanon (anti-Israeli, anti-Western), not on direct support to Iran's war effort. Hezbollah conducted operations aligned with Iranian interests but in its own theater. Restraint from joining the Iran-Iraq front directly: ~8 years (full war duration).

**Lithuania re: Ukraine (2022-present):**
Despite strong rhetorical support, Lithuania declined Ukrainian requests to deploy forces into Ukraine. NATO allies maintained non-intervention despite being the patron coalition. Duration: 4+ years and ongoing.

### Cases Where Proxies Escalated

**Hezbollah after October 7, 2023:**
Began cross-border operations with Israel within days of Hamas's attack. But this was joining a *proxy peer's* fight, not responding to a patron under attack. Iran was not under direct attack at that point. Restraint duration: <48 hours.

**Iraqi Shia militias after Soleimani killing (Jan 2020):**
Iran's patron-figure was directly targeted. Iraqi militias launched rocket attacks on US bases within days. But the sustained campaign was limited — a few weeks of heightened attacks before returning to baseline. Restraint duration: <1 week for initial response, but full escalation was restrained by Iran's decision not to spiral.

**Soviet proxies in 1973 Yom Kippur War:**
Egypt and Syria launched a coordinated attack on Israel with Soviet backing. When Israel counterattacked successfully, the Soviets threatened direct intervention, prompting a US nuclear alert (DEFCON 3). Proxy restraint broke down when the patron's client was losing. Duration of restraint before Soviet escalation threat: ~2 weeks.

## Patterns

1. **Proxies with independent strategic interests tend to show longer restraint.** The Houthis have a Yemen-first agenda; Hezbollah had a Lebanon-first agenda during the Iran-Iraq War. Proxies that are essentially extensions of the patron (Iraqi PMF units) escalate faster.

2. **Depleted capability enforces restraint regardless of intent.** The Houthis' low stockpile after 18 months of Red Sea operations is a hard constraint. Strategic patience is easier when you don't have the ammunition for impatience.

3. **The 30-day threshold appears meaningful.** In cases where proxies chose restraint, the first month is the highest-risk window. If restraint holds past ~30 days, the proxy has likely made a strategic decision to sit out this round. The Houthis are currently at this threshold.

4. **Patron-under-direct-attack is a different trigger than proxy-peer-under-attack.** Proxies respond much faster when a peer proxy is hit (Hezbollah after Oct 7: <48h) than when the patron itself is attacked (Houthis after Iran strikes: weeks+). This is counterintuitive but consistent — patron attacks create confusion about chain of command, while peer attacks create solidarity and immediate tactical opportunities.

## Application to Market Analysis

For markets with 30-45 day resolution windows on proxy escalation:
- If the proxy has independent strategic interests AND depleted capability: lean toward restraint holding
- If the proxy is operationally integrated with the patron AND has capacity: lean toward escalation within 1-2 weeks
- The 30-day mark is the key inflection — restraint that survives a month tends to persist
